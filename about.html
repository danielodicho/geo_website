About Me

The Story Behind Hidden Trails Asheville

Hello! I'm Jamie (they/them), a lifelong Asheville resident and passionate hiker who's been exploring these mountains since childhood. After spending years discovering the lesser-known trails and natural wonders around Western North Carolina, I decided to create this blog to share these hidden gems with others who love nature as much as I do.

By day, I work as an environmental educator, teaching local students about the rich biodiversity and ecological importance of the Southern Appalachians. On weekends and evenings, you'll find me on the trails with my rescue dog, Maple, capturing photos and taking notes for this blog.

My Hiking Philosophy

I believe that the best hiking experiences often happen off the beaten path. While popular destinations like Looking Glass Rock and Max Patch are undeniably beautiful, there's something magical about finding a secluded waterfall or mountain vista that feels like your own personal discovery.

That said, I'm deeply committed to responsible outdoor ethics. I practice and promote Leave No Trace principles and encourage all visitors to treat these special places with respect.

What You'll Find Here

On Hidden Trails Asheville, I share:

  • Detailed guides to lesser-known hiking trails
  • Seasonal recommendations (best wildflower hikes, fall color spots, winter views)
  • Photography tips for nature enthusiasts
  • Local wildlife and plant identification guides
  • Conservation news affecting our local natural areas
