Cedar Rock Mountain Trail

Moderate 4.5 miles 800 ft elevation 3-4 hours 45 min from Asheville

Hidden in plain sight within the popular Pisgah National Forest, Cedar Rock Mountain offers one of the best panoramic views in the region without the crowds that flock to nearby Looking Glass Rock.

Trail Highlights

  • Expansive granite rock face with 180° views
  • Unique plant communities in rock crevices
  • Old-growth forest sections
  • Wildlife viewing opportunities

Best Time to Visit

Spring for wildflowers and fall for foliage, but the exposed rock face makes this a great year-round hike on clear days. Summer mornings offer the most comfortable temperatures.

Crowd Level

Low You'll likely see only a handful of other hikers, even on weekends

Why I Love This Trail

Cedar Rock Mountain has been my go-to recommendation for visitors who want to experience the grandeur of Pisgah National Forest without battling the crowds at more famous spots. The first time I hiked here, I couldn't believe that such an impressive view could remain relatively unknown.

What makes this trail special is the combination of diverse forest environments leading to the expansive granite face at the summit. Unlike many mountain hikes that only offer views at designated overlooks, Cedar Rock's bald summit allows you to wander across the exposed rock and find your own perfect spot to take in the scenery.

I've hiked this trail in all seasons, and each offers something unique: spring wildflowers in the forest sections, summer thunderstorms rolling across the valley (though I don't recommend being on the exposed rock during storms!), fall's kaleidoscope of colors, and winter's crisp, clear views of distant snow-capped peaks.

Trail Description

The Cedar Rock Mountain Trail begins at the Pisgah Center for Wildlife Education parking area. Look for the Cat Gap Loop trailhead near the fish hatchery. You'll follow Cat Gap Loop for approximately 0.7 miles before turning right onto the Cedar Rock Trail.

The junction of Cat Gap Loop and Cedar Rock Trail is marked with this sign

The trail climbs steadily through a beautiful hardwood forest with rhododendron thickets. After about a mile on the Cedar Rock Trail, you'll begin to encounter patches of exposed granite. These are just appetizers for what's to come, but they offer nice spots to catch your breath.

As you continue climbing, the forest gradually gives way to more exposed rock. Around the 2-mile mark from the trailhead, you'll emerge onto the main rock face of Cedar Rock Mountain. The trail is marked by small cairns (rock piles) across the granite, but navigation is generally intuitive—just keep heading up.

The rewarding view from Cedar Rock's summit on a clear autumn day

The summit area is expansive, with plenty of space to find a private spot for a picnic or meditation. On clear days, you can see Looking Glass Rock, John Rock, and even Mount Pisgah in the distance. The exposed granite retains heat, making this a surprisingly comfortable spot even on cooler days.

For the return journey, you have two options:

  1. Out-and-back: Return the way you came for a total of about 4.5 miles.
  2. Loop option: Continue across the rock face to connect with the Big Rock Trail, which will eventually lead you back to the Cat Gap Loop and to your starting point. This adds about 1.5 miles to your hike but offers new scenery.
Trail map showing both the out-and-back and loop options

Getting There

From downtown Asheville, take I-26 East to Exit 40 for NC-280/Airport Road. Follow NC-280 West for about 16 miles to the intersection with US-276 in Pisgah Forest. Turn right onto US-276 and continue for approximately 5.5 miles into Pisgah National Forest.

Look for the Pisgah Center for Wildlife Education/Fish Hatchery sign on your right. Turn right and follow this road to the parking area at the end. The trailhead is located near the education center.

Parking Tip

The parking area can fill up on weekends, especially during summer and fall. Arrive before 9 AM for the best chance at a spot. There is no fee for parking or trail access.

What to Bring

Essentials

  • At least 2 liters of water per person
  • Trail snacks or lunch for the summit
  • Navigation (map, AllTrails download)
  • First aid kit
  • Sun protection (the rock face has no shade)

Recommended

  • Hiking boots with good traction for the granite
  • Trekking poles for the steeper sections
  • Camera (the views are spectacular)
  • Binoculars for spotting wildlife
  • Light jacket (it can be windy at the summit)

Local Wildlife and Plants

Cedar Rock Mountain hosts a diverse ecosystem, particularly around the granite outcroppings where specialized plant communities thrive in shallow soil pockets.

Flora Highlights

  • Piedmont Rhododendron (blooms May-June)
  • Rock Tripe Lichen (covering many granite surfaces)
  • Galax (ground cover with red fall color)
  • Mountain Laurel (blooms late spring)

Fauna Highlights

  • Peregrine Falcons (occasionally spotted)
  • Eastern Fence Lizards (sunning on rocks)
  • Black Bears (uncommon but present)
  • Various Warblers (spring migration)

Insider Tips

Best Photography

Visit in late afternoon for the best lighting on the rock face and surrounding mountains. Morning fog in the valley below can also create magical conditions for photography.

Extended Adventure

For a full day of hiking, combine this trail with nearby John Rock for a challenging 8-mile adventure with two of the best views in Pisgah.

Local Refuel

After your hike, stop at The Hub in Pisgah Forest for craft beer and food truck meals, or continue to Brevard for more dining options.

Seasonal Considerations

Spring

Pros: Wildflowers in bloom, comfortable temperatures, waterfalls at peak flow

Cons: Occasional rain showers, trails can be muddy

Summer

Pros: Long daylight hours, lush green forest

Cons: Hot on exposed rock, afternoon thunderstorms common

Fall

Pros: Spectacular foliage, clear views, perfect temperatures

Cons: Slightly more crowded, especially on October weekends

Winter

Pros: Solitude, expanded views through leafless trees

Cons: Cold winds at summit, possible ice on north-facing sections
